# Anti-Patterns and Product Traps

Date de controle: `25 mars 2026`

## Purpose

This document exists to stop SwissBuilding from becoming a large but
forgettable tool.

The market already has enough products that are:

- broad but shallow
- impressive but not adopted
- data-rich but workflow-poor

## Trap 1 - Becoming a better folder system

Failure mode:

- documents are indexed better
- search is nicer
- dashboards are nicer
- the user still has to interpret everything manually

Why it fails:

- information is present
- understanding is not produced
- action is not driven

Corrective rule:

- every important document must improve a workflow, blocker view, proof state,
  or next action

## Trap 2 - Building a second system for every concept

Failure mode:

- second permit engine
- second obligation entity
- second action system
- second document flow

Why it fails:

- duplicated truth
- wiring cost rises
- users lose trust in which state matters

Corrective rule:

- extend canonical anchors
- project into existing objects
- only create a new primitive when it unlocks a real gate

## Trap 3 - Confusing coverage with usefulness

Failure mode:

- more rules
- more jurisdictions
- more data fields
- no clearer answer to `what applies, what blocks, what next`

Why it fails:

- users do not buy breadth alone
- they buy reduced uncertainty and reduced rework

Corrective rule:

- every added rule source must improve explainability, blocker visibility,
  proof clarity, or routing

## Trap 4 - Beautiful UI without operating habit

Failure mode:

- product looks polished
- nobody develops a daily reflex to open it

Why it fails:

- no operating inbox
- no urgent queue
- no repeated value loop

Corrective rule:

- always ask: what is the daily or weekly habit this surface creates

## Trap 5 - Procedure theater

Failure mode:

- procedure labels exist
- no real step state
- no deadline linkage
- no complement request trace

Why it fails:

- the workflow is still managed outside the app

Corrective rule:

- if a procedure concept cannot affect blockers, obligations, proof, or
  routing, it is not a real product gain yet

## Trap 6 - Fake authority readiness

Failure mode:

- export a PDF and call it authority-ready

Why it fails:

- no procedure context
- no request-response trace
- no acknowledgement
- no confidence about version or completeness

Corrective rule:

- authority readiness means dossier + route + trace + delivery + response loop

## Trap 7 - Demo magic with no production truth

Failure mode:

- wow screen works only in one seeded path
- real product cannot reproduce the same value

Why it fails:

- buyers notice the gap later
- team starts protecting demo fiction instead of product truth

Corrective rule:

- every demo reveal must be usable outside the demo

## Trap 8 - Benchmarking without proof grounding

Failure mode:

- scorecards and benchmarks appear early
- underlying workflow truth is weak

Why it fails:

- score loses credibility
- users cannot act on it

Corrective rule:

- benchmark only what can be traced back to proof, obligations, blockers, or
  procedure state

## Trap 9 - Trying to replace every incumbent directly

Failure mode:

- SwissBuilding tries to be ERP, chantier suite, DMS, analytics suite, deal
  room, and CRM at once

Why it fails:

- product focus collapses
- validation cost explodes
- adoption slows down

Corrective rule:

- win by becoming the canonical building workspace
- integrate the rest

## Trap 10 - Automating ambiguity instead of exposing it

Failure mode:

- low-confidence local or regulatory cases are forced into deterministic paths

Why it fails:

- wrong confidence
- silent legal or procedural mistakes

Corrective rule:

- explicit `manual_review_required` beats silent false precision

## Final rule

When in doubt, prefer:

- clarity over breadth
- projection over duplication
- workflow value over metadata
- proof reuse over one-off output
- visible ambiguity over fake certainty
