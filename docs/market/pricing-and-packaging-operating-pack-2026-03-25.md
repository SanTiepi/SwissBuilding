# Pricing and Packaging Operating Pack

Date de controle: `25 mars 2026`

## Purpose

Compress the current strategy into pricing and packaging rules that match the
actual wedge instead of a vague "all-in-one platform" pitch.

This pack is meant to guide product and sales shaping, not to lock final price
points prematurely.

## Hard rule

Package around buyer value already visible in the product:

- safe-to-start dossier
- proof and completeness
- actor-ready packs
- living building memory
- portfolio escalation later

Do not package around abstract future infrastructure.

## Packaging ladder

### Package 1 - Wedge

Buyer thinks:

- complete pre-work pollutant dossier

Buyer gets:

- evidence chain
- completeness and missing-piece logic
- actor-ready packs
- traceable building memory

Best fit:

- `VD/GE` property managers
- real pre-work dossiers

### Package 2 - Operational

Buyer thinks:

- living building dossier and contributor workflow

Buyer gets:

- continuity across projects
- reduced documentary rework
- traceability across actors
- reusable proof over time

Best fit:

- repeat operators
- public owners
- multi-actor teams

### Package 3 - Portfolio

Buyer thinks:

- risk, cost, and sequence intelligence

Buyer gets:

- cross-building prioritization
- blocker and obligation aggregation
- benchmark and learning leverage later

Best fit:

- multi-building managers
- larger portfolios

## Pricing posture

Keep the posture:

- overlay-first
- low switching-cost entry
- deeper value expansion over time

Avoid positioning that implies:

- ERP replacement on day one
- legal compliance guarantee
- infrastructure pricing before workflow value is proven

## Product implications

The product should visibly support the package ladder with:

- wedge-specific first-run surfaces
- buyer-specific pack presets
- portfolio mode only when the account really earns it

## Acceptance

This pack is useful when the commercial story stays as disciplined as the
product wedge.
