# BatiConnect - Deck Outline (8 Slides)

## Slide 1 - Title / Thesis

**BatiConnect**

Building readiness layer for regulated pre-work pollutant dossiers.

Speaker note:

- We are building the proof and readiness layer between fragmented building documents and real go/no-go decisions before works start.
- We are not replacing property ERPs or diagnostic firms.

## Slide 2 - Problem

Headline:

**Documented does not mean ready.**

Core points:

- pollutant dossiers are spread across emails, drives, ERP attachments, lab annexes, and PDFs
- managers often discover missing or outdated proof too late
- outcomes are authority returns, rework, delayed starts, and weak diligence trace

Speaker note:

- The pain is operational and repeated, not theoretical.

## Slide 3 - Buyer and wedge

Headline:

**Start with the narrowest painful workflow.**

Core points:

- buyer: technical lead in a multi-building property manager
- geography: `VD/GE` first
- use case: pre-work pollutant dossier readiness
- promise: know what is proven, what is missing, and whether the dossier is ready for the authority

Speaker note:

- This is intentionally not a broad real-estate software pitch.

## Slide 4 - Product

Headline:

**Readiness layer + proof engine**

Core points:

- makes blockers and missing proof explicit
- distinguishes documented vs procedurally ready
- keeps provenance visible
- generates a usable authority-oriented dossier flow

Speaker note:

- No legal guarantee, no ERP replacement, no opaque AI claim.

## Slide 5 - Why now / Why this is hard to solve with generic tools

Headline:

**Generic systems store. They do not qualify readiness.**

Core points:

- ERP: system of record, not procedural readiness
- drives / SharePoint: storage, not proof logic
- lab reports: expert outputs, not owner-side continuity
- chantier tools: execution-heavy, not pre-work pollutant readiness

Speaker note:

- The gap is between storage and decision-grade readiness.

## Slide 6 - Moat

Headline:

**The moat compounds with usage.**

Core points:

- canton rule packs: `VD -> GE -> FR -> VS`
- evidence chain and provenance
- living building memory
- feedback flywheel from real dossier corrections

Speaker note:

- The moat does not come from AI alone. It comes from regulatory depth, memory, and trust.

## Slide 7 - Current state and plan

Headline:

**Product ahead of proof; next truth is commercial.**

Core points:

- product wedge and doctrine are now locked
- company is pre-PMF
- next target: `3 paid pilots` on `1` scenario
- motion: `service-led paid design partner`

Speaker note:

- The risk is not lack of product; it is the proof gap.

## Slide 8 - 12-month milestones

Headline:

**From pilots to repeatable motion**

Core points:

- `Q2 2026`: sign `2` paid pilots
- `Q3 2026`: convert `1-2` to annual subscriptions, add `FR`
- `Q4 2026`: reach `4-5` recurring clients, publish `1` case study
- `Q1 2027`: prove repeatable sales-to-pilot-to-conversion loop

Close line:

If BatiConnect wins this wedge, it earns the right to build the broader building memory and readiness platform. If it fails, the answer is not more breadth. It is tighter proof.
