# SwissBuildingOS - European-Model, Swiss-Launch Strategic Market Memo

## 1. Executive takeaway

SwissBuildingOS should not be framed as a canton-first niche tool.
It should be framed as a **European Building Evidence Layer and future Building Intelligence Network**, launched in Switzerland because Switzerland offers the strongest combination of:
- aging stock
- regulatory intensity
- strong public building identifiers and data infrastructure
- wealthy and documentation-heavy asset operators
- a realistic path from building-level proof to portfolio intelligence

The correct strategy is therefore three-layered:

- **European model**
  - the long-term category is European in shape
  - the product model should be designed to generalize across markets
- **Swiss operating layer**
  - Switzerland is the launch market because it is data-rich, regulation-rich, and commercially credible
- **Cantonal rules packs**
  - cantons are execution packs, not the category frame
  - `VD` and `GE` come first because they make the wedge explicit

### Category now

**SwissBuildingOS is the Building Evidence Layer for regulated renovation readiness.**

### Category later

**SwissBuildingOS becomes a Building Intelligence Network / Agent OS for buildings and portfolios.**

### Commercial wedge now

**VD/GE-first, amiante-first, AvT -> ApT safe-to-start dossier**, sold first to **multi-building property managers** as an **overlay above ERP and document systems**.

### Core recommendation

Lock 5 near-term strategy decisions:
- build for a **European category**, not a canton-specific product
- launch in **Switzerland first**
- execute first through **Vaud and Geneva**
- sell first to **multi-building property managers**
- win on **proof, completeness, provenance, dossier packaging, and post-works building memory**

## 2. Swiss and European market reality

### Why Europe matters as the real category frame

The bigger opportunity is not uniquely Swiss.
Europe is entering a structural building-transition era driven by:
- renovation pressure
- energy and carbon regulation
- documentation and auditability expectations
- fragmented building information
- rising importance of portfolio-level decision support

The European Commission's revised Energy Performance of Buildings Directive (`EPBD`, EU/2024/1275) reinforces that this is not a local niche:
- it entered into force on `28 May 2024`
- it pushes renovation, decarbonisation, digitalisation, and technical assistance
- it strengthens the logic of building data, lifecycle logic, and structured information over time  
  Source: [European Commission - EPBD](https://energy.ec.europa.eu/topics/energy-efficiency/energy-efficient-buildings/energy-performance-buildings-directive_en)

Europe also increasingly recognizes the logic of **digital building logbooks** and structured building repositories, which supports the long-term SwissBuilding category thesis.  
Sources:
- [BUILD UP - openDBL and digital building logbooks](https://build-up.ec.europa.eu/en/news-and-events/news/improving-buildings-efficiency-opendbl)
- [European Commission / BUILD UP references to digital building logbooks](https://build-up.ec.europa.eu/sites/default/files/content/bpie_wlc_policy-brief_final.pdf)

Inference:
- the **product model should be Europe-shaped**
- the **launch sequence should be Swiss**

### Why Switzerland is the right launch market

Switzerland is still the best launch market because it has unusually favorable conditions:

1. **Aging and renovation-relevant stock**
   - Federal statistics indicate about `1.79 million` residential buildings and `4.79 million` dwellings in 2023, with a large share of the stock built before the late twentieth century.  
     Sources:
     - [FSO/BFS - Building and Dwelling Statistics press release](https://dam-api.bfs.admin.ch/hub/api/dam/assets/32329330/master)
     - [FSO/BFS - Building and Dwelling Register](https://www.bfs.admin.ch/bfs/en/home/registers/buildings-dwellings.html)
   - Suva states that nearly three quarters of Swiss buildings were built before the 1990 asbestos ban.  
     Source: [Suva - Asbestos in buildings](https://www.suva.ch/en-ch/material/asbestos-in-buildings)

2. **Buildings are central to Swiss energy and climate pressure**
   - OFEN states that Swiss buildings account for about `40%` of final energy use and around `one third` of CO2 emissions.  
     Source: [OFEN/BFE - Buildings](https://www.bfe.admin.ch/bfe/en/home/efficiency/buildings.html)

3. **Renovation is structurally encouraged**
   - OFEV documents the Buildings Programme and the federal impulse programme in force since `1 January 2025`.  
     Source: [OFEV/BAFU - Funding in the building sector](https://www.bafu.admin.ch/en/funding-in-the-building-sector)

4. **Public building data infrastructure is unusually strong**
   - `GWR / RegBL`
   - `MADD`
   - `swissBUILDINGS3D`
   - cantonal geodata ecosystems
   These make building identity, geospatial anchoring, and future digital-twin logic highly plausible in Switzerland.  
     Sources:
     - [FSO/BFS - Building and Dwelling Register](https://www.bfs.admin.ch/bfs/en/home/registers/buildings-dwellings.html)
     - [MADD public access](https://www.housing-stat.ch/en/madd/public.html)
     - [swisstopo - swissBUILDINGS3D](https://www.swisstopo.admin.ch/en/height-model-swissbuildings3d)

### Why `VD/GE` are the best first execution zones

`VD` and `GE` should be treated as the first **rules packs**, not as the market frame itself.

They are the best first execution zones because they make the pre-work pollutant workflow explicit:

- **Vaud**
  - explicit `AvT` / `ApT` logic
  - `GDA`-style documentary and process expectations
  - formal distinction between permit and non-permit workflows  
    Sources:
    - [Etat de Vaud - Diagnostic et assainissement](https://www.vd.ch/territoire-et-construction/amiante-1/diagnostic-et-assainissement)
    - [Etat de Vaud - Complements a la directive](https://www.vd.ch/territoire-et-construction/amiante-1/complements-a-la-directive)

- **Geneva**
  - strong owner-facing before-work guidance for amiante and plomb
  - clear operational trigger for documentation and proof collection  
    Sources:
    - [ge.ch - owner workflow before works](https://www.ge.ch/renover-transformer-demolir-sans-danger-amiante-pcb-plomb/je-suis-proprietaire-immobilier)
    - [ge.ch - directive diagnostic plomb avant travaux](https://www.ge.ch/document/directives-plomb-diagnostic-travaux-peintures-contenant-du-plomb)

Inference:
- launch in Switzerland
- execute first in `VD/GE`
- then extend by adding new **rules packs** canton by canton

## 3. Initial buyer and wedge

### Primary buyer

Primary buyer:
- **multi-building property managers**

Why they beat diagnosticians as the first buyer:
- they own project coordination across many buildings
- they suffer directly from delay, stop-work, rework, and incomplete proof
- they must arbitrate readiness before works start
- they are the natural bridge to portfolio logic

Diagnosticians, labs, architects, and contractors are strategically important, but they should initially be treated as:
- contributors
- proof suppliers
- users of workflow portals

### Commercial wedge

The first commercial wedge should remain sharply defined:

**VD/GE-first, amiante-first, AvT -> ApT safe-to-start dossier**

That wedge means:
- ingest diagnostics, lab annexes, plans, and supporting files
- structure and link the proof
- identify missing evidence
- orchestrate missing-piece collection
- produce authority-ready, owner-ready, and contractor-ready packs
- preserve post-works memory

### Why this wedge is non-optional

The wedge is anchored in real pre-work obligations and practical risk:
- asbestos investigation before works in relevant buildings
- work interruption and risk escalation when dangerous substances are discovered late
- cantonal and owner-side need for usable and defensible dossiers

Relevant official sources:
- [Suva - Asbestos in buildings](https://www.suva.ch/en-ch/material/asbestos-in-buildings)
- [Suva - Renovation and asbestos guidance](https://www.suva.ch/fr-ch/prevention/par-danger/materiaux-rayonnements-et-situations-a-risque/amiante/identifier-les-produits-contenant-de-lamiante/dans-quelles-parties-des-batiments-trouve-t-on-de-lamiante)
- [Etat de Vaud - Diagnostic et assainissement](https://www.vd.ch/territoire-et-construction/amiante-1/diagnostic-et-assainissement)
- [ge.ch - owner workflow before works](https://www.ge.ch/renover-transformer-demolir-sans-danger-amiante-pcb-plomb/je-suis-proprietaire-immobilier)
- [OFSP/BAG - Renovation and dangerous substances](https://www.bag.admin.ch/fr/renovation-attention-aux-substances-dangereuses)

### ERP posture

SwissBuilding should be sold as an **overlay above ERP, archives, and existing management tools**.

Why:
- managers already operate systems of record
- switching cost is high
- the shortest route to adoption is to become the layer that:
  - structures evidence
  - evaluates dossier readiness
  - packages proof
  - preserves cross-project building memory

### EGID as the preferred building anchor

Recommendation:
- use `EGID` as the preferred dossier anchor whenever a trustworthy mapping exists
- keep secondary references alongside it:
  - address
  - local IDs
  - parcel / cadastral references where available
  - cantonal references

That keeps the product:
- Swiss-native in execution
- Europe-compatible in model design

## 4. Competitive landscape

### Current category entry point

Today, SwissBuilding is entering:

**regulated renovation readiness and building evidence workflow**

not yet the full operating-system category.

### What gap still exists

There is still no obvious dominant Swiss product that combines:
- regulated pre-work proof
- evidence links
- living building memory
- dossier generation
- portfolio escalation

### The competitive framing that matters

SwissBuilding should avoid being mistaken for:
- a generic document tool
- a chantier task tracker
- a portfolio decarbonization planner alone
- a property ERP

It should be positioned as:

**the Building Evidence Layer that sits between regulatory/document reality and operational/portfolio decisions**

See `docs/market/competitor-landscape.md` for the structured map.

## 5. Category thesis

### Category now

**Building Evidence Layer for regulated renovation readiness**

Why this is the right claim now:
- it maps to a real trigger
- it maps to a real buyer
- it maps to legal/documentary pain
- it is more precise and sellable than “Agent OS” on day one

### Category later

**Building Intelligence Network / Agent OS**

This becomes credible once SwissBuilding proves:
- reusable building memory
- reliable evidence chain
- visible reduction in documentary rework
- portfolio learning across repeated projects and buildings

### Category boundary

SwissBuilding should not present itself as:
- a generic CDE
- a generic BIM platform
- a generic AI assistant
- a decarbonization dashboard only

It is the layer that can feed those systems with structured, trusted building evidence.

## 6. Moat assessment

### Demo wow

Easy-to-copy wow:
- PDF summaries
- AI explanations without provenance
- static dashboards
- ungrounded "chat with your building" ideas

These are not moat.

### Real moat now

The strongest moat candidates now are:

1. **regulatory workflow depth**
   - OTConst / Suva logic
   - cantonal rules packs
   - pollutant-specific readiness workflows

2. **evidence chain**
   - every score / field / action linked to proof

3. **living building memory**
   - especially `AvT -> ApT` continuity

4. **overlay adoption**
   - usable without replacing ERP

### Future moat if adoption compounds

The strongest later moat candidates are:
- data network effects from diagnostics, interventions, and completed dossiers
- portfolio learning effects
- invisible agents embedded in real workflows
- Europe-ready rules-pack extensibility on top of a Swiss-proven evidence core

## 7. GTM implications for the roadmap

### Product implication

The market implies the roadmap should prioritize:
- parser + extraction
- completeness engine
- evidence chain
- pack generation
- post-works memory / `AvT -> ApT`
- interop layer
- later portfolio steering on top of proven evidence

### Packaging logic

#### Wedge package

Buyer thinks they buy:
- a complete pre-work pollutant dossier

Buyer actually gets:
- evidence chain
- completeness reasoning
- building memory
- next actions

#### Operational package

Buyer thinks they buy:
- a living building dossier and contributor workflow

Buyer actually gets:
- continuity across projects
- reduced documentary rework
- auditability
- stronger readiness before works

#### Portfolio package

Buyer thinks they buy:
- risk/cost/sequence intelligence across buildings

Buyer actually gets:
- a Building Evidence Layer that can feed portfolio, ESG, and Capex tooling

### Ideal PoC partner profile

Ideal first PoC partner:
- property manager active in `VD` and/or `GE`
- multiple renovation-relevant older buildings
- real near-term renovation work
- willingness to run one project end-to-end
- willingness to involve diagnostician/lab contributors
- willingness to measure dossier readiness outcomes

### PoC success metrics

The wedge becomes compelling only if a real project shows:
- completeness `>= 95%`
- documentary rework reduction `>= 50%`
- time to authority-ready pack `<= 2h`
- provenance integrity `= 100%`
- reusable post-works memory

## 8. Risks and weak points

1. **Cantonal fragmentation**
   - solved by rules packs, not by pretending one canton equals all of Switzerland

2. **Adjacent workflow vendors can speak about legal certainty**
   - SwissBuilding must stay anchored in pollutant evidence and readiness logic

3. **Liability framing**
   - do not claim legal compliance guarantee
   - claim completeness, provenance, and traceable orchestration

4. **ERP integration depth is still unspecified**
   - start with links, exports, archives, and light connectors

## 9. Ideal claims now / claims to avoid

### Claims to make now

- SwissBuildingOS helps determine whether a renovation dossier is ready to move forward
- it structures pollutant-related evidence before works
- it reduces fragmentation and documentary rework
- it creates a reusable building dossier
- it overlays existing management systems rather than replacing them

### Claims to avoid for now

- “we guarantee legal compliance”
- “we replace your ERP”
- “we are already the national infrastructure”
- “AI alone solves the problem”

## 11. Recommendation

Lock these decisions:

1. build for a **European category**
2. launch in **Switzerland first**
3. execute through **VD/GE rules packs first**
4. sell first to **multi-building property managers**
5. build moat around:
   - regulatory depth
   - evidence chain
   - living building memory
   - overlay adoption

If the PoC cannot demonstrate completeness, provenance, rework reduction, and reusable post-works memory on a real project, the wedge is not yet strong enough and product work should continue to focus on extraction, evidence, and rules before broader expansion.
