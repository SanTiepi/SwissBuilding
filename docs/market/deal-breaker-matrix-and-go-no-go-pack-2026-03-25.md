# Deal-Breaker Matrix and Go/No-Go Pack

Date de controle: `25 mars 2026`

## Purpose

Not every prospect or workflow deserves equal effort.

This pack defines the main deal-breakers that should change product priority,
pilot design, or sales posture.

## Rule

If a buyer or use case fails on multiple deal-breakers, SwissBuilding should:

- narrow scope
- pilot one slice only
- or walk away

## Deal-breakers

### 1. No canonical building anchor available

Risk:

- identity remains too weak

### 2. Buyer only wants generic document storage

Risk:

- category drift
- low moat

### 3. No willingness to centralize procedure or proof

Risk:

- SwissBuilding becomes decorative

### 4. No recurring workflow habit exists

Risk:

- low adoption

### 5. Buyer expects day-one full ERP replacement

Risk:

- rollout friction too high

## Go posture

Good fit:

- regulated or document-heavy process
- need for clarity and proof reuse
- multi-actor coordination pain
- real blocker visibility value

## Acceptance

This pack is succeeding when the team can say:

- where SwissBuilding is a strong fit
- where a bounded pilot is needed
- where it is better not to force a deal
