# SwissBuildingOS - ICP and GTM Hypotheses

This file is the GTM sequencing artifact for Swiss-first go-to-market.
The strategy follows a double reading:
- Europe-shaped category ambition
- Swiss-first launch, with `VD/GE` as the first execution wedge

## P1 - Property managers in VD/GE

### Buyer

- multi-building property managers
- technical building managers
- owner-side operational managers with recurring renovation exposure

### Trigger

- renovation / transformation / demolition works
- permit or pre-work pollutant obligations
- fear of stop-work or rework due to late discovery
- missing or fragmented documentation before works

### Current workflow

- collect PDF diagnostics, lab annexes, plans, and emails
- validate whether the evidence fits the real work scope
- coordinate experts and contributors manually
- archive outputs into ERP / drives with weak reuse

### Pain severity

- `5`

### Budget owner

- technical property-management lead
- renovation project owner
- operations lead on the asset side

### Likely champion

- renovation project manager
- technical property manager
- building-risk/compliance-minded operations lead

### Likely blocker

- ERP inertia
- archive/process inertia
- liability concerns

### Buying process

- pilot one real project
- standardize SOP
- expand to more buildings
- expand to multi-pollutant and portfolio use

### Strongest message

**Before works start, know what is proven, what is missing, and whether the dossier is safe to move forward.**

### Recommended priority

- `P1`

## P2 - Owners / small funds

### Buyer

- private owners with repeated renovation exposure
- family offices
- smaller funds or owner groups

### Trigger

- renovation decisions
- acquisitions or due diligence
- refinancing / risk review
- repeated dependence on external actors with fragmented documentation

### Current workflow

- rely on managers, diagnosticians, and architects
- store building knowledge in fragmented places
- rediscover the same issues at each project

### Pain severity

- `3`

### Budget owner

- owner representative
- asset manager
- capital-allocation owner for the project

### Likely champion

- owner-side decision-maker
- asset manager
- trusted advisor

### Likely blocker

- lower repetition than professional managers
- price sensitivity
- assumption that the manager already handles the problem

### Buying process

- often enters through a concrete building or through a manager-led process

### Strongest message

**Turn one-off pre-work evidence into a reusable building memory that protects value and reduces surprises.**

### Recommended priority

- `P2`

## P2/P3 - Public owners / municipalities

### Buyer

- municipalities
- public building departments
- public portfolio owners

### Trigger

- renovation of public assets
- auditability and traceability requirements
- need to preserve knowledge despite turnover

### Current workflow

- formal and multi-actor processes
- fragmented documentation across departments and external parties
- long approval cycles

### Pain severity

- `4`

### Budget owner

- public property / infrastructure department
- technical building service
- renovation-program owner

### Likely champion

- patrimoine bati lead
- public portfolio manager
- technical building head

### Likely blocker

- procurement complexity
- security and data-governance requirements
- long decision cycles

### Buying process

- likely after references in P1
- more suitable after the wedge is proven and packaged

### Strongest message

**Standardize proof, preserve memory, and reduce documentary friction across public building projects.**

### Recommended priority

- `P2`

## P3 - Diagnosticians / labs as contributor ecosystem

### Buyer role

- not the primary initial commercial wedge
- important contributors and workflow users

### Trigger

- repeated rework on deliverables
- client requests for structured output
- desire to reduce missing-piece back-and-forth

### Current workflow

- create reports and annexes
- send PDFs
- respond to follow-up requests
- lose structured reusability downstream

### Pain severity

- `2`

### Budget owner

- practice lead
- quality lead
- operations lead

### Likely champion

- founder
- senior diagnostician
- QA lead

### Likely blocker

- smaller tool budgets
- intermediation concerns
- fragmented market structure

### Buying process

- best introduced through P1 demand:
  - structured upload
  - contributor portal
  - request-missing-pieces workflows

### Strongest message

**Deliver once in a structured way and make your proof immediately usable by the client.**

### Recommended priority

- `P3`

## Optional later-stage segment - institutions

This segment is real but post-wedge:
- institutional owners
- insurers
- funds

Recommendation:
- keep them in the strategic horizon
- do not optimize the initial GTM motion for them

## GTM sequence

1. run one real `VD` or `GE` project end to end
2. standardize the `AvT -> ApT safe-to-start dossier` SOP
3. deploy across multiple buildings for the same manager
4. expand from amiante-first to multi-pollutant coverage
5. expand into public owners
6. expand into portfolio intelligence

## Packaging logic

### Wedge package

Buyer thinks they buy:
- a complete pre-work pollutant dossier

Buyer actually gets:
- evidence chain
- completeness engine
- next actions
- reusable building memory

### Operational package

Buyer thinks they buy:
- a living building dossier and contributor workflow

Buyer actually gets:
- continuity across projects
- reduced documentary rework
- stronger auditability

### Portfolio package

Buyer thinks they buy:
- risk/cost/sequence intelligence across buildings

Buyer actually gets:
- a Building Evidence Layer that feeds later portfolio, ESG, and Capex logic

## GTM principles

- do not lead with generic AI
- do not lead with “national infrastructure” claims
- do not lead with ERP replacement
- do lead with:
  - readiness before works
  - proof and provenance
  - reduced rework
  - reusable post-works memory
