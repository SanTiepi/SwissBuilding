# Buyer Objection Map and Counterproof Pack

Date de controle: `25 mars 2026`

## Purpose

SwissBuilding should not only have features.

It should have prepared answers to the objections that stop adoption.

This pack maps likely objections to the product proof that must answer them.

## Rule

An objection is only really answered when the product can show:

- what the user gets
- what changes operationally
- what risk disappears

## Objection 1 - "We already have an ERP"

Real concern:

- duplication
- another tool to maintain

Counterproof:

- SwissBuilding overlays ERP instead of replacing it on day one
- canonical building truth and procedural clarity live here
- existing back office can remain where it is

Product proof surfaces:

- ControlTower
- packs
- diagnostics publication
- proof reuse

## Objection 2 - "This will be too much work to set up"

Real concern:

- migration pain
- retraining
- no time

Counterproof:

- one workflow can start without full replacement
- imports preserve provenance
- one queue or one pack can create value immediately

Product proof surfaces:

- switching cost pack
- seeded demo scenarios
- importer and handoff flows

## Objection 3 - "Our team already works with email and drives"

Real concern:

- habit inertia
- people do not want another workspace

Counterproof:

- SwissBuilding replaces re-explaining and dossier rebuilding, not just storage
- proof delivery and packs reduce email chaos directly
- the system answers what matters now and what is blocked

Product proof surfaces:

- building overview
- ControlTower
- ProofDelivery
- handoff pack

## Objection 4 - "Can we trust this for authorities and regulated work?"

Real concern:

- proof defensibility
- wrong route
- incomplete dossier

Counterproof:

- traceable source and version
- visible confidence and manual-review states
- procedure and complement loops modeled explicitly

Product proof surfaces:

- Authority Flow
- confidence ladder
- decision traces
- proof trust layer

## Objection 5 - "We do not want a generic document tool"

Real concern:

- many tools promise structure but do not drive workflow

Counterproof:

- SwissBuilding turns documents into blockers, obligations, procedures, packs,
  and proof reuse

Product proof surfaces:

- must-win workflows
- proof reuse scenarios
- canonical timeline

## Objection 6 - "Communal and cantonal differences are too messy"

Real concern:

- local exceptions
- fear of false automation

Counterproof:

- pilot commune strategy
- confidence ladder
- explicit manual review
- no false national automation claim

Product proof surfaces:

- pilot commune candidates
- communal adapter projection map
- review queue

## Objection 7 - "What happens if we stop using it?"

Real concern:

- lock-in fear

Counterproof:

- exports and exchange remain first-class
- evidence, versions, and provenance are portable
- product wins by accumulated usefulness, not artificial captivity

Product proof surfaces:

- passport exchange
- transfer package
- proof delivery records

## Acceptance

This pack is succeeding when the team can answer each objection with:

- one sentence
- one workflow
- one visible proof surface
