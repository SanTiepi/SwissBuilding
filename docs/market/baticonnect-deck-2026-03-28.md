# BatiConnect - Deck Draft

## Slide 1 - Title

**BatiConnect**

Building readiness layer for regulated pre-work pollutant dossiers

Subline:

From fragmented documents to authority-ready decisions before works start.

Speaker notes:

- We are not replacing property ERPs, diagnostic firms, or document storage.
- We are building the proof and readiness layer between fragmented building information and real go/no-go decisions.

## Slide 2 - The problem

**Documented does not mean ready.**

- pollutant dossiers live across emails, drives, ERP attachments, lab annexes, and PDFs
- technical managers often discover missing, outdated, or contradictory proof too late
- the result is authority returns, documentary rework, delayed starts, and weak diligence trace

Speaker notes:

- The market already has storage and workflow tools.
- What it lacks is procedural readiness clarity before regulated works.

## Slide 3 - The first wedge

**Start where the pain is repeated and budgeted.**

- buyer: technical lead in a multi-building property manager
- geography: `VD/GE` first
- workflow: pre-work pollutant dossiers before authority submission or tender
- promise: know what is proven, what is missing, and whether the dossier is ready

Speaker notes:

- This is intentionally not a broad real-estate software pitch.
- The wedge is narrow enough to sell and deep enough to compound.

## Slide 4 - The product

**Readiness layer + proof engine**

- imports diagnostics and existing documents
- evaluates completeness and readiness
- detects missing, outdated, or contradictory proof
- turns gaps into concrete next actions
- packages a traceable authority-oriented dossier

Speaker notes:

- No legal guarantee.
- No ERP replacement.
- No autonomous AI decision claims.

## Slide 5 - Why generic tools do not solve this

**Storage is not readiness.**

| Existing tool | What it does well | What it does not do |
|---|---|---|
| ERP | system of record | procedural readiness |
| Drive / SharePoint | storage and sharing | missing-proof logic |
| Lab portal | report delivery | owner-side continuity |
| Construction workflow tools | execution coordination | pre-work pollutant readiness |

Speaker notes:

- The gap is not access to documents.
- The gap is decision-grade clarity before works start.

## Slide 6 - Why this can become a moat

**The moat compounds with usage.**

- canton rule packs: `VD -> GE -> FR -> VS`
- evidence chain with provenance
- living building memory
- feedback flywheel from real dossier corrections

Speaker notes:

- The moat does not come from AI alone.
- It comes from regulatory depth, memory, and trust.

## Slide 7 - Current state

**Product ahead of proof; next truth is commercial.**

- wedge, pricing, motion, and canton order are now locked
- company is pre-PMF
- main risk is the `proof gap`, not lack of product depth
- next objective: `3` paid pilots on `1` scenario

Speaker notes:

- We should be honest about stage.
- The next milestone is not more product breadth.
- It is paid usage and conversion evidence.

**From pilots to repeatable motion**

- `Q2 2026`: sign `2` paid pilots and establish baseline scorecards
- `Q3 2026`: convert `1-2` to annual subscriptions and add `FR`
- `Q4 2026`: reach `4-5` recurring clients and publish `1` case study

Closing line:

If BatiConnect wins this wedge, it earns the right to become the trusted readiness and memory layer for building workflows. If it fails, the answer is not more breadth. It is tighter proof.
