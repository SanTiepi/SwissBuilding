# BatiConnect - Investor Memo (EN)

## Thesis

BatiConnect is building a narrow but commercially credible wedge: the readiness and proof layer for regulated pre-work pollutant dossiers in Switzerland, starting with multi-building property managers in Vaud and Geneva. It is not trying to replace property ERPs, diagnostic firms, or generic document systems. It sits above fragmented documents, public data, expert reports, and existing archives to answer one critical operational question before works start: what is proven, what is missing, and is this dossier actually ready to move forward with the authority?

If that wedge works, it can open a larger moat around canton-specific rule packs, living building memory, and reusable proof across projects and actors. The ambition can be large. The execution posture is deliberately narrow.

## Why this problem matters now

The current workflow is structurally weak. Pre-work pollutant dossiers are spread across emails, drives, ERP attachments, lab annexes, diagnostic PDFs, and informal handovers between experts and managers. A dossier can look documented while still being procedurally unready. The consequence is not just inconvenience; it is authority returns, documentary rework, delayed starts, and weak evidence that the right diligence was performed at the right time.

The first buyer is clear: the technical lead inside a multi-building property manager. This is the person who has to coordinate fragmented evidence before renovation, transformation, or demolition work moves ahead. The pain is concentrated enough to sell, repeated enough to matter, and credibility-sensitive enough that generic software does not solve it.

This is why the entry wedge matters. BatiConnect is not entering through broad ESG reporting, portfolio analytics, or a vague "AI for buildings" story. It is entering through a non-optional moment where documentary ambiguity creates operational and financial risk.

## What BatiConnect sells now

BatiConnect sells a regulated pre-work pollutant dossier workflow for `VD/GE` property managers. The promise is simple: before works start, the client can know what is proven, what is missing, and whether the dossier is ready for the authority. The right category today is a readiness layer plus a proof engine.

That framing is intentionally disciplined. BatiConnect does not claim to provide legal certification. It does not claim to replace the client's ERP. It does not recommend which remediation company to choose. It does not present AI as an autonomous decision-maker. AI is used where it creates leverage and remains reviewable, but the commercial promise is not "AI magic." The promise is clarity, traceability, and reduced documentary rework before regulated works.

## Why this can compound

The long-term opportunity does not depend on feature breadth. It depends on a small number of assets that get stronger with real usage.

First, BatiConnect can accumulate canton-specific rule packs. Regulatory depth is hard to fake because buyers only trust it when it maps to the actual local workflow. The planned sequence is explicit: `VD`, then `GE`, then `FR`, then `VS`.

Second, BatiConnect can build an evidence chain with provenance. The product does not just store files; it ties sources, versions, contradictions, missing pieces, and downstream use into one auditable layer. That matters because the buyer's real problem is not storage. It is defensible understanding.

Third, BatiConnect can become the living memory of a building. A report that normally dies as a PDF can become a reusable proof asset attached to one building over time. This creates legitimate retention because value compounds through history rather than through artificial lock-in.

Fourth, BatiConnect can learn from real dossier corrections. The real flywheel is not abstract AI intelligence. It is the repeated codification of extraction corrections, readiness exceptions, missing-piece patterns, and local workflow variants.

The moat therefore does not come from AI alone. It comes from the combination of regulatory depth, building memory, and traceable proof.

## Current state

The product is currently ahead of its commercial proof. That is the central near-term risk. The main risk is not lack of product capability. It is the proof gap: the possibility of having a sophisticated system without yet proving that buyers will pay, adopt, and repeat.

Several core decisions are now locked:

- wedge: pre-work pollutant dossiers for `VD/GE` multi-building managers
- category: readiness layer plus proof engine
- pricing posture: paid pilot first, then annual organization subscription with dossier quota
- go-to-market motion: service-led design partner, never free
- canton sequence: `VD -> GE -> FR -> VS`
- cultural principle: no verdict without explainable provenance

What is not yet proven is product-market fit. There is no reason to pretend otherwise. The next truth to earn is simple: three paid pilots on one scenario.

## 12-month path

`Q2 2026`: sign two paid pilots and establish clean baselines with scorecards.

`Q3 2026`: convert at least one to two pilots into annual subscriptions and add `FR` as the third regulatory anchor.

`Q4 2026`: reach four to five recurring clients and publish at least one case study reusable in sales.

`Q1 2027`: prove that the sales-to-pilot-to-conversion motion repeats.

Three validation milestones matter more than vanity metrics:

- if paid pilots do not happen quickly, the wedge or motion is wrong
- if pilots happen but do not convert, value is not being felt by the sponsor
- if conversion happens without repeated use, the workflow habit and memory advantage are still too weak

## Main risks and near-term decisions

The first risk is the build trap, expressed here as the proof gap: building faster than the market learns to buy. The second risk is that BatiConnect may become very good at revealing holes without becoming the tool people open every week to resolve them. The third risk is dispersion outside the wedge, especially into attractive but premature areas such as broad portfolio analytics, generalized ESG narratives, or custom product requests.

The corresponding decisions should remain firm:

- stay service-led on the first pilots
- offer more service, never more custom product
- protect the principle that no verdict is allowed without explainable provenance

That is the point of discipline: a narrow wedge, a strong trust posture, and a short path to commercial truth. If BatiConnect succeeds there, the broader ambition becomes much more credible. If it does not, the right response is not to widen the story. It is to tighten the loop between product, proof, and buyer value.
