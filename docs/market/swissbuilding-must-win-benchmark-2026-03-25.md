# SwissBuilding Must-Win Benchmark (2026-03-25)

## Purpose

This document answers one hard product question:

**If strong products already exist, where does SwissBuilding need to be clearly better, or it is not worth building?**

The goal is not to deny that other products are good.
The goal is to be explicit about:
- what they already solve well
- where they still leave structural pain in the Swiss building workflow
- which capabilities SwissBuilding must win on
- which categories we should integrate with instead of trying to out-ERP them

This benchmark is based on:
- current repo strategy docs
- official or vendor public pages accessed on `2026-03-25`
- official/public digital-service and building-passport references

## Core thesis

SwissBuilding should not try to win by being:
- a better chantier task tool than `smino`, `PlanRadar`, `Dalux`, or `Procore`
- a better Swiss property ERP than `GARAIO REM`, `W&W`, or `AbaImmo`
- a better portfolio analytics stack than `Wuest Dimensions`, `Scandens`, or `Optiml`
- a better consumer homeowner content/tool platform than `Houzy`

SwissBuilding is worth building only if it becomes better at the intersection that others still leave broken:

**one building, one canonical workspace, one procedural truth, one evidence chain, many actors, many audiences, reusable over time**

That is the gap.

## What already exists and what that means

### 1. Construction collaboration and site execution are already strong categories

Relevant players:
- `smino`
- `PlanRadar`
- `Dalux`
- `Procore`

What they already do well:
- central project communication
- task and defect management
- 2D/BIM plan access
- versioning and traceability
- field capture, photo capture, diaries, inspections, acceptances
- collaboration between office and site teams

Evidence:
- `smino` positions itself as an intuitive construction management platform from planning to key handover, with CDE, task management, defect management, guarantee phase, inspections, and public-owner/building-authority surfaces. It also claims `27,000+` companies on its public site.
- `PlanRadar` presents `170,000+` users across `75+` countries, 2D plans and BIM models, automatic versioning, open API/webhooks, and strong documentation/defect/reporting workflows.
- `Dalux` explicitly pushes a BIM-centered `CDE`, ISO `19650` information management, and integrated design/field/document flows.
- `Procore` pushes unlimited document storage, revision control, permissions, project linking, and a large integration ecosystem.

Implication:
- If SwissBuilding only offers tasks, defects, plans, diaries, and generic collaboration, it will look like a weaker copy of an existing category.
- We should reuse lessons from these tools:
  - instant mobile capture
  - location-aware documents
  - strict version visibility
  - low-friction collaboration
  - audit trail by default

But SwissBuilding must go further on:
- Swiss regulatory readiness
- procedural blockers
- owner/authority/fiduciary-grade packaging
- long-lived building memory beyond one project

### 2. Swiss property management ERPs are deeply entrenched

Relevant players:
- `GARAIO REM`
- `W&W`
- `AbaImmo`

What they already do well:
- contracts, rents, accounting, fees, owner communication, process digitalization
- installed workflows and migration gravity
- daily operational legitimacy for Swiss property managers

Evidence:
- `GARAIO REM` says more than `1.9 million` objects are managed daily by `200+` customers, and offers owner portal, invoice processing, on-site app, and digital acceptance protocols.
- `W&W` emphasizes `45+` years of market presence and an ecosystem for digital property management.
- `AbaImmo` presents itself as a complete property-management solution, with automated reminders, technical management, digital document storage, dashboards, signatures, and public figures of `480` companies, `1,000,000` managed units, and `3,700` daily users.

Implication:
- SwissBuilding should not position itself as "the new ERP replacing everything on day one".
- The right posture remains:
  - overlay
  - integrator
  - evidence/procedure/workspace layer
- We win when these systems remain the ledger or back office, while SwissBuilding becomes the place where the building is actually understood, shared, made ready, and proven.

### 3. Portfolio intelligence and decarbonization are already credible battlegrounds

Relevant players:
- `Wuest Dimensions`
- `Scandens`
- `Optiml`

What they already do well:
- portfolio steering
- scenario analysis
- renovation/decarbonization planning
- institutional decision support

Evidence:
- `Wuest Dimensions` publicly highlights `11,000` active users, `600` clients, around `300 bn` of assets under management, and broad coverage across investment planning, construction planning, portfolio analysis, risk, taxes, and sustainability.
- `Scandens` frames renovation planning as transparent, data-driven, and easy for owners and advisors, with a strong decarbonization narrative.
- `Optiml` is already recognized in our repo strategy as a real long-term adjacency in renovation optimization and decarbonization planning.

Implication:
- If SwissBuilding only becomes a planning/scenario/ESG dashboard, it enters a strong category without enough differentiation.
- SwissBuilding must ground portfolio intelligence in something these players are typically weaker on:
  - legally and procedurally usable evidence
  - document/procedure continuity
  - building-level proof reuse
  - actor coordination around an actual file, not only a portfolio model

### 4. Homeowner simplicity is already being attacked from below

Relevant players:
- `Houzy`
- `CLÉA`
- `Woningpas / Gebouwenpas`

What they already do well:
- owner-friendly language
- simple maintenance and renovation guidance
- document centralization
- sharing building information with third parties
- "digital home twin" or "digital passport" mental model

Evidence:
- `Houzy` claims `150,000+` users and `235,000+` digital home twins, with renovation calculators, maintenance guidance, smart to-do logic, property valuation, solar/heating tools, and expert/craftsman routing.
- `CLÉA` claims `250,000+` homes equipped in France and sits on top of a real legal obligation: the `Carnet d'Information du Logement`.
- `Woningpas` and `Gebouwenpas` in Flanders are official digital passports that centralize property information and can be shared with buyers, tenants, architects, contractors, and notaries.

Implication:
- If SwissBuilding is hard to understand or only made for experts, homeowner-facing adoption will lose to simpler tools.
- The benchmark here is not "more features".
- The benchmark is:
  - clearer next actions
  - better trust
  - easier sharing
  - less jargon
  - better continuity through time

## The actual white space

The white space is not empty software territory.
It is a **broken workflow territory** between existing systems.

What is still structurally broken:
- the same building is split across ERP, drive folders, emails, PDFs, plan tools, expert reports, authority exchanges, and mental memory
- documents exist, but nobody knows if the dossier is actually procedurally ready
- the same evidence must be rebuilt for owner, authority, contractor, insurer, fiduciary, or buyer
- responsibilities are unclear across actors
- official Swiss restrictions, identifiers, and public data are rarely operationalized into everyday action
- project tools stop when the chantier ends, but the building memory should continue

This is where SwissBuilding can be category-defining.

## Must-win criteria

SwissBuilding deserves to exist only if it can become excellent on these points.

### 1. One canonical building file across time

Must beat:
- fragmented project tools
- static document folders
- one-project-only collaboration spaces

Definition of win:
- a building keeps its history across interventions, tenants, owners, diagnosticians, authorities, and contractors
- the file survives project boundaries
- the next project starts with context, not from zero

### 2. Procedural readiness, not just document storage

Must beat:
- generic DMS/CDE tools
- generic portals that only collect files

Definition of win:
- SwissBuilding can tell the difference between:
  - documented
  - incomplete
  - blocked
  - procedurally ready
  - approved
  - expired
- every blocker is explicit
- every missing item is linked to a source, requirement, authority, or step

### 3. Once-only evidence reuse

Must beat:
- repeated upload and resend cycles
- dossier recomposition by audience

Definition of win:
- one piece of proof can be reused for:
  - authority pack
  - transfer pack
  - insurer/fiduciary pack
  - contractor handoff
  - future renovation context
- version, provenance, and delivery are preserved

This should follow the same spirit as the EU "once-only" principle:
- users should not have to re-provide what the system can already retrieve or reuse with the right permissions and traceability

### 4. Swiss official-data depth by default

Must beat:
- manual lookup culture
- screenshots and ad hoc geodata checks

Definition of win:
- the building workspace is anchored on real identity and restrictions:
  - `EGID`
  - `EGRID`
  - address
  - public restrictions and cadastre context
  - canton/commune/authority routing
- Swiss public sources improve action quality instead of sitting as reference PDFs

### 5. One operating inbox for the building

Must beat:
- scattered to-do lists
- buried reminders inside ERP
- passive dashboards

Definition of win:
- the first useful screen answers:
  - what blocks progress
  - what expires soon
  - what is missing
  - who must act
  - what should be sent next
- this works at:
  - building level
  - portfolio level
  - organization/user queue level

### 6. Better authority experience, not just owner experience

Must beat:
- authority interactions by email only
- opaque complement loops
- unclear version history

Definition of win:
- the authority sees a clean procedural file
- complement requests are explicit, timestamped, and linked to evidence
- submissions, acknowledgements, and responses are traceable
- the dossier is easier to assess, not just easier to upload

### 7. Multi-actor coordination without role confusion

Must beat:
- "everybody sees everything" document shares
- unstructured email forwarding

Definition of win:
- owner, manager, diagnostician, contractor, fiduciary, insurer, notary, and authority can share the same building workspace without collapsing privacy or scope
- each actor sees:
  - relevant context
  - assigned actions
  - necessary evidence
  - no more

### 8. Homeowner-grade simplicity with professional-grade rigor

Must beat:
- rigid ERPs on usability
- consumer tools on regulatory/procedural depth

Definition of win:
- a private owner can understand it
- a gérance can run a portfolio with it
- an authority can trust it
- a diagnostician can publish into it

### 9. Packs and deliveries as first-class product objects

Must beat:
- "download a zip and hope"
- undocumented email sends

Definition of win:
- SwissBuilding knows:
  - who received what
  - when
  - in which version
  - by which method
  - whether it was viewed or acknowledged

### 10. Switzerland-deep, Europe-shaped

Must beat:
- canton-specific dead ends
- generic global software that never goes deep enough

Definition of win:
- SwissBuilding is deeply Swiss in:
  - rules
  - identifiers
  - authorities
  - procedures
  - language
  - document expectations
- but architected to generalize later as a wider building evidence and procedure model

## The five frictions we must kill first

These are the highest-value frictions still left unsolved by the market intersection.

### 1. Invisible procedure

Users may have documents, but they still do not know:
- whether the dossier is ready
- what authority expects next
- what blocks a project from moving

### 2. Dead documents

Most documents are stored, but not operationalized:
- no required/optional distinction
- no procedural attachment
- no version significance
- no delivery history
- no cross-audience reuse

### 3. Rebuilt dossiers

The same building story gets reconstructed repeatedly for:
- authority
- contractor
- insurer
- fiduciary
- future sale or transfer

### 4. Lost accountability

Even when teams collaborate, it is often unclear:
- who owns the next step
- who owes a response
- who must provide proof
- what is overdue versus blocked

### 5. Broken continuity

Most tools are either:
- project tools
- ERP tools
- consumer guidance tools

Very few become the persistent building workspace that survives from first diagnosis to works, transfer, operation, future interventions, and authority interactions.

## Anti-goals

SwissBuilding should avoid these traps.

### Trap 1: "Let's build a Swiss PlanRadar"

Why it fails:
- the category already exists
- those tools are already strong at tickets, defects, plans, and site documentation

### Trap 2: "Let's replace Swiss ERPs first"

Why it fails:
- installed base, accounting gravity, migration pain, and back-office depth are too strong

### Trap 3: "Let's only be a dashboard"

Why it fails:
- dashboards do not become indispensable without action, proof, and workflow consequences

### Trap 4: "Let's only be a homeowner advice platform"

Why it fails:
- then we lose the professional/procedural wedge
- Houzy and similar tools already make the surface approachable

### Trap 5: "Let's only be a regulatory encyclopedia"

Why it fails:
- users do not want a rule library
- they want to know what applies, what is missing, what blocks, and what to do

## Product tests to apply before shipping

Before committing to a major surface, ask:

### If this is mainly a collaboration tool
- Is it meaningfully better than `smino`, `PlanRadar`, or `Dalux` on the target use case?
- If not, should SwissBuilding integrate instead of compete?

### If this is mainly a back-office function
- Is it worth attacking `GARAIO REM`, `W&W`, or `AbaImmo` now?
- If not, should we treat it as an integration boundary instead?

### If this is mainly portfolio analytics
- Is it grounded in building-level evidence and procedure, or is it just another scenario layer?

### If this is mainly owner UX
- Is it simpler than `Houzy` and at least as trustable as a public/official passport model like `Woningpas` or `CLÉA`?

### If this is mainly regulatory
- Does it drive an obligation, blocker, route, pack, or next action?
- If not, it is probably still documentation, not product.

## What "better than existing" should mean in practice

The minimum acceptable product bar is:

### Better than project tools on continuity
- SwissBuilding must outlive a chantier

### Better than ERPs on clarity
- SwissBuilding must make the building understandable, not only manageable

### Better than portfolio tools on proof
- SwissBuilding must tie strategy to auditable building evidence

### Better than homeowner tools on seriousness
- SwissBuilding must be genuinely useful in regulated, multi-actor, document-heavy reality

### Better than public passports on actionability
- SwissBuilding must not stop at "information available"
- it must produce blockers, obligations, procedures, packs, routing, and reusable proofs

## Immediate implications for current roadmap work

This benchmark reinforces the current direction:
- `WorkspaceMembership` matters because multi-actor scope is core, not optional
- `DocumentInbox` matters because dead incoming documents are still a major friction
- `Obligation` matters because deadlines are a must-win axis
- `PermitProcedure` matters because procedure is still the biggest structural gap
- `ControlTower` matters because action routing is the habit-forming surface
- `ProofDelivery` matters because pack sending without traceability is not enough
- `SwissRules` matters because SwissBuilding only wins if rules become operational, explainable, and up to date

It also sets hard constraints:
- do not build a second permit logic next to `permit_tracking`
- do not build a second action system next to `ControlTower`
- do not build a second obligation concept
- do not build a generic DMS that forgets procedure, evidence, and reuse

## Bottom line

SwissBuilding is only worth pursuing if it becomes:

**more continuous than project tools, clearer than ERPs, more grounded than portfolio software, more serious than homeowner apps, and more actionable than public building passports**

Anything below that is incremental software, not a category-worthy product.
