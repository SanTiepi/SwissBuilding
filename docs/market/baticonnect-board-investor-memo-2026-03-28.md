# BatiConnect - Building readiness layer for regulated pre-work pollutant dossiers

BatiConnect is building a narrowly defined but commercially serious wedge: the readiness and proof layer for regulated pre-work pollutant dossiers in Switzerland, starting with multi-building property managers in Vaud and Geneva. It is not trying to replace property ERPs, diagnostic firms, or generic document systems. It sits above fragmented documents, public data, expert reports, and existing archives to answer one operationally critical question before works start: what is proven, what is missing, and is this dossier actually ready to move forward? If this wedge works, it opens a larger moat around rule packs, building memory, and reusable proof.

## Why now / Why this problem matters

The pain is not theoretical. In the current workflow, pre-work pollutant dossiers are spread across emails, drives, ERP attachments, laboratory annexes, diagnostic PDFs, and informal handovers between experts and managers. A dossier can look documented while still being procedurally unready. The result is authority returns, documentary rework, delayed start dates, and weak evidence that the right diligence was performed at the right time.

For the first wedge, the buyer is clear: the technical lead inside a multi-building property manager. This is the person who has to coordinate fragmented evidence before renovation, transformation, or demolition work moves ahead. The pain is concentrated enough to be sold, repeated enough to matter, and credibility-sensitive enough that generic software does not solve it.

This is why the entry point matters. BatiConnect is not entering through broad ESG reporting, portfolio dashboards, or a generalized "AI for buildings" message. It is entering through a non-optional moment where documentary ambiguity creates operational and financial risk.

## What we sell now

What BatiConnect sells now is deliberately simple: a regulated pre-work pollutant dossier workflow for `VD/GE` property managers. The core promise is that, before work starts, the client can know what is proven, what is missing, and whether the dossier is ready for the authority. The category is best described as a readiness layer plus a proof engine.

That positioning keeps the product honest. BatiConnect does not claim to provide legal certification, replace the client's ERP, or recommend which remediation company to choose. It does not present AI as an autonomous decision-maker. AI is used where it creates leverage and remains reviewable, but the promise is clarity, traceability, and reduced documentary rework before regulated works.

The strength of the wedge is that it is narrow enough to sell and deep enough to compound. A client can buy it for one dossier or a small set of buildings, but the value naturally extends toward repeated use, shared habits, and portfolio learning.

## Why this can compound

The long-term opportunity does not depend on feature breadth. It depends on a small number of assets that get better with real usage.

First, BatiConnect can accumulate canton-specific rule packs. Regulatory depth is hard to fake because buyers only trust it when it maps to the actual local workflow. The expansion sequence is explicit: `VD`, then `GE`, then `FR`, then `VS`.

Second, BatiConnect can build an evidence chain with provenance. The product does not just store files; it ties sources, versions, contradictions, missing pieces, and downstream use into one auditable layer.

Third, BatiConnect can become the living memory of a building. A report that normally dies as a PDF can become a reusable proof asset attached to one building over time. This creates legitimate retention because value compounds through history, not through artificial lock-in.

Fourth, BatiConnect can learn from real dossier corrections. The real flywheel is not abstract AI intelligence. It is the repeated codification of extraction corrections, readiness exceptions, missing-piece patterns, and local workflow variants.

The moat therefore does not come from AI alone. It comes from the combination of regulatory depth, building memory, and traceable proof.

## Current state and honesty

The product is currently ahead of its commercial proof. That is the central risk. The main near-term risk is not lack of product capability. It is the proof gap: the possibility of having a sophisticated system without yet proving that buyers will pay, adopt, and repeat.

Several core decisions are now locked. The wedge is fixed. The buyer is fixed. The pricing posture is fixed: paid pilot first, then annual organization subscription with dossier quota. The motion is fixed: service-led design partner, never free. The provenance principle is fixed: no verdict without explainable evidence. The canton sequence is fixed: `VD -> GE -> FR -> VS`.

What is not yet proven is product-market fit. There is no reason to pretend otherwise. The next truth to earn is simple: three paid pilots on one scenario. Until that happens, BatiConnect should behave like a disciplined pre-PMF company, not like a company that has already won its category.

## Next 12 months

`Q2 2026`: sign two paid pilots and establish clean baselines. The goal is not volume. It is proof that the same scenario can run on real dossiers with a scorecard the buyer respects.

`Q3 2026`: convert at least one to two pilots into annual subscriptions and add `FR` as the third rule-pack anchor. If pilots remain interesting but non-converting, the issue is not roadmap breadth; it is value perception, sponsor quality, or buying motion.

`Q4 2026`: reach four to five recurring clients and publish at least one case study that can be reused in sales. This is the point where the company should start to see whether BatiConnect can become a repeated path of least resistance.

`Q1 2027`: prove that the motion repeats. The bar is not a larger product. The bar is a tighter sales-to-pilot-to-conversion loop.

Three validation milestones matter more than any vanity metric. If paid pilots do not happen quickly, the wedge or motion is wrong. If pilots happen but do not convert, the value is not being felt by the sponsor. If conversion happens without repeated use, the workflow has not yet become a habit and the memory advantage is still theoretical.

## Main risks and near-term decisions

The first risk is the build trap, expressed here as the proof gap: building faster than the market learns to buy. The second risk is that BatiConnect may become very good at revealing holes without becoming the tool people open every week to resolve them. The third risk is dispersion outside the wedge, especially into broad portfolio analytics, generalized ESG narratives, or custom requests that break focus.

The corresponding decisions should remain firm. BatiConnect should stay service-led on the first pilots. It should offer more service, never more custom product. And it should protect the principle that no verdict is allowed without explainable provenance.

That combination is the point of discipline: a narrow wedge, a strong trust posture, and a short path to commercial truth. If BatiConnect succeeds there, the broader ambition becomes much more credible. If it does not, the right response is not to widen the story. It is to tighten the loop between product, proof, and buyer value.
